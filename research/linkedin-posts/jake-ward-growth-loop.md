## Post Content Breakdown & Strategic Core

### Executive Summary of the Post:
"Most founders think AI SEO is dead because of Google Core Updates. It’s not dead; your generic prompting is what's dead. Here is the exact 'Information Gain' framework we used to scale a B2B SaaS client to 1.2M monthly impressions using automated clustering paired with human Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)."

### Key Actionable Frameworks Highlighted:

1. **The Semantic Map vs. Keyword Lists:**
   * Stop looking for single high-volume keywords on Ahrefs. Instead, map out **Topical Hubs**.
   * Use LLM APIs to cluster 500 semantic variations of a single core intent (e.g., "B2B invoicing software features", "automated billing systems for tech teams").
   * Build 1 pillar article and 15 supporting micro-articles. Link them all back to the pillar. This signals absolute topical authority to Google’s algorithm.

2. **The "Information Gain" Prompt Formula:**
   * Never prompt an LLM with just: *"Write a 1500-word blog post about X."*
   * Ground the prompt using internal data. Jake shares his injection prompt structure:
     ```text
     "Analyze the attached internal CSV containing our product feature data and the transcript of our last interview with the product manager. Write the section 'How to Optimize Invoicing Workflows' utilizing ONLY the proprietary data provided. Do not use generic internet facts."
     ```
   * Result: The content passes Google's automated quality filter because it introduces net-new information that doesn't exist anywhere else on the web.

3. **The ROI of the Hybrid Model (AI + Human SME):**
   * Traditional writer cost: $250 per article (takes 2 days).
   * Hybrid model cost: $50 to generate the programmatic grounded foundation + $70 for a Subject Matter Expert to review, add screenshots, and refine technical jargon (takes 30 minutes).
   * This allowed his agency to scale production by **10x** while increasing conversion rates on the blog by 34% because the content actually speaks the language of corporate decision-makers.
